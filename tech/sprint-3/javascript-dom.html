JavaScript & DOM

HTML, CSS and Javascript analogy

Think about your favourite food, and imagine that you want to cook it for your family. So, its base ingredients would be the HTML of your recipe. The different extra things you want to add to it, to make it taste the way you like, the spices you want to use, those my friend would be your CSS. And finally, the stove, the oven, and all the cookware that allows you to play with the ingredients, changing their shape and texture, or the cutlery that allows your family to interact with your food and change its shape, all of that would be the Javascript.

Control flow, functions and loops

Control flow is basically the order in which the computer executes instructions from top to bottom in a script.

Functions are one of the most fundamental building tools in Javascript, they are like automated orders that you create, so every time you use them your computer does what you expect. They are very helpful, as they can be used multiple times.

And loops are a sequence of these instructions that is continually repeated until a specific condition is reached.

I know it may sound confusing, so let’s try to explain it with something more familiar. Let’s say you are the kind of person who showers every day - doesn’t matter if you are not, the example would work anyway, I’m not judging by the way -. Ok, so the normal process of a shower, taking away all the extra things you like or may need to do before, is like turning on the extractor/fan, getting in the shower, turning on the water, shampoo, soap, rinse, dry.

So, the process of having the shower, starting by turning on the extractor is like the control flow of a program. It goes from top to bottom in that order, you wouldn’t dry before you rinse yourself, or you wouldn’t rinse before you use shampoo or soap, that’s basically that idea.

The action of having it, saying to yourself: “Ok mate, I think we need one!”, the showering perse, that would be the function, it allows this process of having a shower to run from one point to the end. And there is where loops are used, they tell this function to end at a certain point. The loops basically stop you from jumping again in the shower and doing the function over and over again, you wouldn’t shower again just after you dried yourself, well maybe if you had an “accident”, but as I said before, I’m not judging.
