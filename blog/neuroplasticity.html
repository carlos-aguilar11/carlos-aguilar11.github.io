What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the brain’s ability to change and reorganise itself on a cellular level as the result of our interactions with our environment, behaviour, and internal experiences. This ability is what allows our brain to rewire and reorganise itself, both in structure and functionality.

You may think that everything sounds quite obvious, well, understanding the principles of neuroplasticity helps your brain to do these changes in a faster and more efficient way. Understanding how your brain works and how you can improve it, benefits your learning capacity and make pathways formed in your brain and its connections much stronger.

Neuroplasticity, its principles and application

Some of the benefits of neuroplasticity have been defined by different experts and we could resume them as:

  • Learn and Improve: Increasing your memory and cognitive abilities,helping you learn new skills or enhancing existing capabilities.

  • Recover: Helping your brain to recover from mental illnesses, strokes and other injuries.

  • Mental strength and fitness: Helping your brain to rewire functions in areas that are damaged and heighten other senses.

How can I apply all of this to my personal journey of learning? I think one of the most common enemies of somebody who is learning a new skill is themself, this is well-known as the impostor syndrome. Knowing the principles and benefits of neuroplasticity gives me the certainty that my brain is capable of learning and improving itself, rewiring new neuro-paths and has the ability to adapt and enhance in different areas.

But, understanding neuroplasticity and how your brain works won’t be enough, you need to do some changes in your lifestyle if you really want to reach your full potential. Some of the ways to improve it are:

  • Improve your environment,

  • Stimulate your brain with new activities,

  • Exercise regularly,

  • Play games and activate your brain,

  • Get plenty of rest, and

  • Practice Mindfulness

Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is simply the belief that you can improve your abilities through strategies, effort and determination. In contrast, fixed mindset establishes that intelligence, talent and any other human qualities are unchangeable.

The term was mentioned for the first time by American psychologist Professor Carol Dweck in her book Mindset: The New Psychology of Success, 2006. Her exploration and studies show that those who have a growth mindset, meaning they believe that they can develop their talents and capabilities, tend to achieve more than those with a fixed mindset.

Having a growth mindset, helps you to see more opportunities to challenge yourself to improve your talents and develop different skills. Understanding this concept, blending it with your understanding of neuroplasticity and applying all this new knowledge to your learning plan will be fundamental to achieving your full potential.
