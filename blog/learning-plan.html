Plan


7 April 2023

What is your long-term goal or career pathway?

I always like to set short and medium-term goals. I feel like they are more achievable and they help me to focus and go step by step, instead of getting lost in the final goal. Of course, finishing the boot camp and starting a job in tech or coding would be my short-term goal, but soon after that, I want to be a professional web and app developer.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

Strengths:

  1. Proactive
  2. Diligent
  3. Organized
Limitations:
  1. Lack of self-discipline
  2. The need to fix everything on the way.
  3. Language: Even though I can communicate and everything, English is still my second language and that makes it a limitation sometimes.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

In any context that involves any kind of relationship, working, studying, or socialising or every time I’m part of any new group of people, I always try to grow in empathy, synergy and respect, and I would love to use this opportunity to do the same.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I’m going to do my best using my strengths not just only to help my own career development, but also to be available and help anybody who needs me. Being proactive, organized and respectful. Always work as a team and be in constant communication. I will also keep working on my limitations, and improving every day so they don’t affect me or any of my peers. If I’m feeling overwhelmed or not finding the inspiration to get things done, I will always take a step back, try to look at the problem in a different way and if I need it, ask for help.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way and what you expect from the Facilitation team.

I don’t consider myself a ‘stressy’ person, I always try to see different perspectives on any issue in order to resolve it. But, I’m not afraid to ask for help when I need it, so if I feel like I’m not coping with something I will always reach for help. The only thing I expect from the Facilitation team is respect and availability.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

Nope, everything is sorted out. The only thing I have going are physio appointments, but I always plan them with anticipation.

Bootcamp

Learning Plan


29 April 2023

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

As I mentioned in my original plan, I usually prefer to set short and medium-term goals. My goal after finishing the course is to grow my skills to become a web and app developer. I’d like to create new and innovative applications, I’d love to work on finding needs in society that could be fulfilled with these tools.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

The biggest strengths I have and could be fundamental at Bootcamp are my ability to work as part of a team and my empathy. On the other hand, my biggest weaknesses that I need to keep working through this part of the course are my need to fix everything and my self-discipline, a limitation that I’ve been working on successfully during Foundations but still needs improvement.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think having to stick to the time suggested to finish a task or “timebox” and maybe having to talk in front of a large group of people in my “second language” are going to be my biggest non-technical challenges during Bootcamp.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to keep working on the same human skills I mentioned previously in my original plan, but I would like to also work on two extra aspects:

  1. My patience. I feel like when it comes to big ideas and complicated projects I’m super patient in explaining every little detail, but when is about tasks or things that my brain perceives as basic and simple I don’t have the same tolerance.
  2. I’d also like to improve my communication skills, again, as English is my second language I don’t have the same confidence to communicate with large groups of people as I do in my mother tongue, Spanish.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect to be supported and patiently listened to. One of the reasons I choose this cohort instead of the online version was because I feel that being there next to the facilitators would give me the chance to reach out easily when I need it and it would allow me to communicate in a more effective way.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp.

I’m going to do my best working as part of a team. I’m going to improve my self-awareness and empathy and used them for my own growth and to help my team's development. I’m going to be proactive, respectful and always try to communicate efficiently with everyone. I will always be there if somebody needs me and if things feel overwhelming for me, I’ll always try to find calm, refresh my ideas, have a break and ask for help when I need it.

Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā

Wellbeing plan




Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā is a metaphor based on four pillars of a wharenui/meeting house. When we look after all four aspects, we look after our hauora/wellbeing. Checking in on the four pillars helps us balance our hauora and support others to balance theirs, too.

These are my plan and activities to cultivate this model.

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Doing small routines of exercises and stretches during the day. Going to the gym/physio once a week. Going for walks every weekend.
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Having breakfast and dinner with my family every day. Having video calls with my parents a couple of times a week.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Meditating a couple of times every day. Doing small routines of exercises and stretches during the day. Taking a couple of steps back if things seem overwhelming.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Expressing gratitude every day. Being empathetic and respectful of other people and our environment.
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Going to the gym/physio once a week. Being empathetic and respectful of other people and our environment. Contemplating the nature around me every morning.
