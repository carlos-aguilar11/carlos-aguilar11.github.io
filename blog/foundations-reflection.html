Reflections

Foundations Core


28 April 2023

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

The core subjects of the foundations part of the course gave me a better understanding of myself and how to improve my development in this new career. Gave me a better understanding of my emotional intelligence, thought me how my learning can improve with the right focus on my strengths and weaknesses and provided me with many tools to make my work easier and more effective.

What is the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?.

They are intrinsically connected aspects of our EQ and they are fundamental in learning and programming, as they should be in any aspect of our life. Our values are the boundaries of our actions and if we are self-aware and empathic, we would be able to understand our own and other people's thoughts, beliefs and feelings. These skills would give us the ability to:

  • Self-reflection: Reflect our own thoughts, feelings and manners would help us gain an understanding of our own actions and would lead us to self-improvement and personal development.

  • Empathy: Understanding our own emotions would help us to recognise similar emotions in others. Being able to see and understand other people’s perspectives would lead us to better relationships and more effective collaboration.

  • Better communication: Having self-awareness and empathy would able us to communicate our feelings and needs effectively, in a clear and assertive way.

  • Improved decision-making: These skills would also allow us to make better decisions when we face a problem because we would have a better understanding of ourselves, the other people involved and the whole situation. Our decision would be aligned with our and other people’s values and needs.

In recap, developing these skills would make a huge difference in our learning and programming journey, it would help us to handle challenging situations, to understand our strengths and weaknesses, and other people's perspectives and needs.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

In my experience working in teams and leading work groups, I’ve always tried to apply many of the things we’ve seen during the core chapters of Foundations. But I was very and pleasantly surprised by all the evidence and studies about neuroplasticity and growth mindset. I found the concepts so interesting and relating them to other aspects of emotional intelligence gave me some amazing insights.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I think that being self-aware is always hard, it obviously doesn’t have the same intensity for everyone, but being totally aware of your capacities and disadvantages and knowing yourself at the deepest level can be tricky not only to achieve it but also to manage it.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because scientific evidence has shown us how important is the development of EQ in any person and how people with high levels of EQ achieve significantly better results in every aspect of their life. We know thanks to those analyses that developing as a programmer with high levels of emotional intelligence would give us skills to solve problems, communicate and collaborate as part of a team in a more efficient way.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not at all! I think that all the subjects we’ve seen during the core part of Foundations are as relevant as the coding techniques we learnt. I can’t emphasise enough how important is to develop your emotional intelligence and how fundamental this can be to becoming a better programmer and a better person in general.
