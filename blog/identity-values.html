My Identity

Values & Strengths


1 April 2023

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Just to set a bit of context, back in my home country I used to work as a casino supervisor/manager. One time (around 11 years ago), a friend of mine was involved in an illegal activity related to her job as a croupier (casino dealer). After the situation was discovered I decided to cut her off from my life and we never went back to being friends. I wasn’t upset about her doing something illegal, my conflict was that she did it while many of her friends were working there and her actions could have caused major problems for other people, and that lack of empathy in a situation that could have changed people’s lives, in a really bad way, I just couldn’t bear it. Today I still feel the same way, my values have evolved but are still pretty much based on the same structure, so when I look back I still think it was the right thing to do.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I definitely owe my values to my parents. My mum is a very ethical and strong woman, who is also religious, and she was always teaching me and my siblings to be empathic, honest and fair. My dad is a self-raised man, he started to work when he was 7 years old and never stopped. Even though he worked most of the time, he was always there for us and he taught us to be persistent, motivated and hard workers.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I consider myself an honest, empathic, organized and kind person, with a good sense of humour. I think my values will help me to work through the course as part of a team, and this learning journey will help me evolve and teach me more than just coding.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Two things that have been a problem in the past and I’ve been trying to improve during this course are my lack of self-discipline and the need to fix everything on the way. They still show up sometimes, but as I go, I’m learning to prioritise tasks in order to get the job done as best I can.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I remember when I was at Uni, I decided to do my thesis with my group of friends. We were quite a smart group and very competitive, but three of them tended to take the “competition” to the next level. So every time we had to take big decisions regarding the investigation approach, perspective, and division of roles, we always hit a wall and took us forever to get to a conclusion. They always wanted to do the best part or in other words, they always thought they could do better than others. At one point we were running out of time and things were getting tight, So I decided to take action and just make the decisions. I explained my argument, and they agreed and we divided our roles giving them reasons why we would work as we did.

I think my approach today to the same situation would be a bit different, because even though the plan succeeded, I based it on the “trying to get things done” approach, without worrying about the learning experience. That meant, that we all knew our subject to the point to sound like expert professionals, but we only touched base on the subjects that the others had worked on, and that could have been catastrophic in a different scenario. So today, I wouldn’t wait until the last minute to speak, I would try to learn as much as I can about all the subjects of my project and I would try to motivate and help the other members of my group to do their best and achieve their goals.
